Gap Analysis: Quantum 1D Models (2026-03-01)

GAP 1: Entanglement Entropy in Integrable Floquet Time Crystals [CONFIDENCE: HIGH]
Paper arXiv:2510.04326 introduces integrable Floquet DTC in 1D using free-fermion BdG model.
It studies fidelity and spin correlations but NEVER computes entanglement entropy in any phase.
For free-fermion model, EE is exactly computable from correlation matrix for N~10000 sites.
Open questions: EE behavior in DTC vs FTC vs OSL vs PM; EE oscillations in DTC; finite-size scaling; entanglement spectrum statistics across Floquet phase diagram.
Verified: Yes - paper explicitly does not study entanglement.

GAP 2: Entanglement Asymmetry for Z2 Symmetry in Integrable TFIM under DQPT [CONFIDENCE: HIGH]
EA studied for U(1) in XY/XXZ chains. For Z2 in random circuits: no QME (Bertini et al. 2024).
For Z2 in INTEGRABLE models under quench inducing DQPTs: NOT STUDIED.
Key question: Do Loschmidt zeros (DQPT critical times) correspond to singular behavior in EA?
Connection between DQPTs and entanglement symmetry restoration is entirely unexplored.
Verified: Yes - no papers found on this specific combination.

GAP 3: Symmetry-Resolved Entanglement in Floquet DTC Phase [CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM-HIGH]
SREE studied for free-fermion quenches (P3) and driven XY chain (arXiv:2412.03654).
The DTC phase has NEVER been characterized by SREE.
Questions: Does SREE oscillate in DTC? Entanglement Mpemba effect in DTC?
Verified: Partially - one recent paper covers non-DTC Floquet case.

GAP 4: Krylov Complexity in Exactly Solvable 1D Floquet Models [CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM]
Krylov extended to Floquet (arXiv:2412.19797) but without exact analytical benchmarks.
Connection between Krylov complexity and Floquet phase transitions (DTC/OSL boundaries) unexplored.
Verified: Partially.

GAP 5: Finite-Rate Quenches in Dimerized XXZ Chain (Kibble-Zurek) [CONFIDENCE: HIGH]
Paper P1 studies sudden quenches only. Explicitly states finite-rate quenches as future work.
The exact solvability might extend to linear ramp quenches.
Verified: Yes - explicitly stated as future direction by authors.
